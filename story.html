Shoji / Sofiane

A life built between silence, movement, code and hope.

My story is not linear. It is made of pressure, rebuilding, curiosity, and the will to create a life that truly fits who I am.

Who I am

I am Sofiane, also known as Shoji (翔司). I am a student, a builder, and someone who has always lived through multiple worlds at once: technology, movement, languages, art, and self-reinvention.

I do not define myself through only one field. I learn through connection. I grow through challenge. I create through emotion, structure, and curiosity.

My turning point

My path in France became difficult in ways that were both academic and personal. I faced pressure, instability, misunderstanding, and moments where I felt disconnected from the environment around me.

Instead of giving up on my future, I started rebuilding it with more intention. I began to think seriously about the kind of place, system, and community where I could truly grow.

Why Japan

Japan is not just a destination for me. It represents a place where I can rebuild myself with clarity, discipline, creativity, and purpose.

What draws me to Japan is not only the culture, language, or aesthetics. It is the possibility of building a meaningful life in an environment that feels more aligned with who I want to become.

Why Keio

Keio represents something rare to me: a space where disciplines meet, where people are encouraged to think beyond one single box, and where a student can connect technology, society, creativity, and real impact.

That is exactly the kind of environment I am looking for. I do not want to only study. I want to contribute, build, collaborate, and grow.

What I want to build

I want to build a future that connects human experience, technology, expression, and international perspective. My goal is not only to succeed academically, but to create projects that matter.

Through this portfolio, I want to show that my journey is already in motion. I am not starting from zero. I am already building, learning, adapting, and preparing for the next stage of my life.
